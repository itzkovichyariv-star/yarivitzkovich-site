abstract: |
  Workplace mistreatment is a pervasive, costly phenomenon. Human bystanders often fail to
  intervene, paralysed by diffusion of responsibility, social cost, and ambiguity.
  This paper proposes that AI systems, properly designed, can act as automated real-time
  bystanders — noticing, naming, and nudging. We develop a conceptual model specifying design
  principles, organizational conditions, and ethical boundaries.
