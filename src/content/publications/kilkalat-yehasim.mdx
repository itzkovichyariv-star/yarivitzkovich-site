abstract: |
  A Hebrew-language volume tracing the dark side of interpersonal interactions in
  contemporary organizations. Workplace incivility — a term the book foregrounds in its
  English form on the back cover — sits at the centre of the account, alongside power
  abuse and the relational harms that accompany the changing pace of modern work.
  The title turns on a single-letter Hebrew swap — כ for ק — that is inaudible in
  modern pronunciation: כלכלת יחסים ("the economy of relationships") and קלקלת יחסים
  ("the ruining of relationships") are near-homophones. The book argues that the two
  are not separable: what an organization ruins, relationally, is part of what it produces.
