abstract: |
  This Springer Briefs volume argues that academic incivility is better understood as a
  consequence of learning-environment design than as a failure of student character. Drawing
  on research in organizational behaviour, educational psychology, and social-emotional
  competence, the authors propose a two-pronged remedy: cultivating SEL competencies in
  faculty and students, and redesigning classroom and institutional environments so that
  civility becomes the path of least resistance.
