abstract: |
  Across interviews (N = 24) and survey data (N = 312), we show that bystanders to workplace
  bullying frequently become secondary targets. We theorise the conditions — group identification,
  leader signalling, normative ambiguity — that predict whether a witness is pulled into, or
  protected from, the cascade.
