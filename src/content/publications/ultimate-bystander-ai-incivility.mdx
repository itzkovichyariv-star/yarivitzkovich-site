  If humans freeze in the face of workplace incivility, can a listening, intervening AI lower the threshold for help? A trust-based framework and research agenda.
abstract: "Workplace mistreatment poses persistent challenges to organizational well-being, yet bystanders — the largest population exposed — are constrained by power dynamics, fear of retaliation, and diffusion of responsibility. Current measurement approaches compound the problem by failing to detect mistreatment in real time. This paper proposes a theoretical framework for AI-driven bystander intervention using natural language processing for real-time detection, grounded in bystander, trust, and human-AI collaboration theory. A qualitative study with eleven mistreatment-experienced individuals reveals stakeholder acceptance under specific trust conditions and a strong preference for sequential AI-then-human intervention. Findings also identify power capture risk as an emergent boundary condition not previously integrated into AI trust frameworks, and extend bystander theory to propose that technological actors can disrupt the escalation cycles that psychological and social barriers persistently inhibit human bystanders from addressing."
