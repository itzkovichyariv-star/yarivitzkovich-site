abstract: "The research examines how emotional intelligence and gender function as personal and social resources in building resilience for individuals who experienced teacher mistreatment during adolescence. Using Conservation of Resource theory, the study finds that men and women differ in their baseline levels of mistreatment exposure and emotional intelligence capabilities. The findings demonstrate that emotional intelligence and gender affect resilience, with gender and the Use of Emotion facet showing interactive effects. The authors conclude this work offers insights into how to mitigate teacher mistreatment and promote more socially sustainable communities that are just and equitable."
