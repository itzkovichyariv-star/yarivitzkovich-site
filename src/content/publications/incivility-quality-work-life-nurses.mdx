abstract: "The study examined how workplace incivility affects nurses' perceptions of ethical climate and work-life quality. Researchers collected quantitative data from 148 nurses and qualitative interviews with 12 nurses and 14 doctors at an Israeli hospital. Key findings showed that witnessing or experiencing incivility affected the nurses' perception of the ethical climate and their work-life satisfaction. The ethical climate partially mediated the relationship between incivility and quality of work-life. The research extends incivility theory beyond individual conflicts, demonstrating its broader organizational effects and supporting the need for clearer conceptualization of incivility as a multidimensional construct."
