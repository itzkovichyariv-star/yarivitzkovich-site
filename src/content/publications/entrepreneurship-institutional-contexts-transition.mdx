abstract: "We examined how regulative, normative, and cognitive institutional environments shape perceptions of entrepreneurial feasibility and desirability across two Israeli kibbutz community types. Using convenient and snowball sampling, we identified entrepreneurial drive indicators among 253 community members in 2010. MANOVA analysis showed that members of wage differential kibbutzim viewed entrepreneurial action as more feasible but not more desirable than egalitarian kibbutz members, emphasizing the importance of considering specific community characteristics in entrepreneurship research."
