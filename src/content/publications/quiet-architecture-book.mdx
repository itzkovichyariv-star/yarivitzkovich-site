  Across six chapters, the book builds a theory of workplace incivility as organizational
  architecture rather than individual failing. Drawing on a decade of empirical work across
  62 organizations, it argues that low-quality leader–member exchanges, ambiguous norms, and
  diffuse accountability are the load-bearing walls of the everyday rudeness we have come
  to expect. The closing chapters offer a redesign agenda for managers, HR practitioners, and
  OD consultants — with worked examples from field engagements.
